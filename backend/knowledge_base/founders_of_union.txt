WAASISI WA MUUNGANO WA TANGANYIKA NA ZANZIBAR

Hati za Muungano zilisainiwa tarehe 22 Aprili 1964 na waasisi wawili wakuu:
Julius Kambarage Nyerere na Sheikh Abeid Amani Karume.

JULIUS KAMBARAGE NYERERE (1922–1999)

Julius Kambarage Nyerere alizaliwa tarehe 13 Aprili 1922 huko Butiama,
Mkoa wa Mara, Tanganyika. Alihitimu elimu ya chuo kikuu kutoka Chuo Kikuu
cha Edinburgh, Scotland — akiwa Mwafrika wa kwanza wa Tanganyika kufanya
hivyo.

Nyerere alianzisha Chama cha Tanganyika African National Union (TANU) mwaka
1954. TANU iliongoza harakati za uhuru wa Tanganyika hadi uhuru ulipatikana
tarehe 9 Desemba 1961. Nyerere alikuwa Waziri Mkuu wa kwanza, kisha Rais
wa kwanza wa Tanganyika baada ya Tanganyika kuwa Jamhuri mwaka 1962.

Tarehe 22 Aprili 1964, Nyerere alitia saini Hati za Muungano na kuwa Rais
wa kwanza wa Jamhuri ya Muungano wa Tanzania. Aliongoza Tanzania hadi
mwaka 1985.

Nyerere alijulikana kwa jina la "Mwalimu" — akiwa mwalimu kabla ya kuingia
siasani. Alipigania umoja wa Afrika, haki ya binadamu, na maendeleo ya
kijamii. Alifanya kazi kubwa ya kupigana dhidi ya ubaguzi wa rangi Afrika
Kusini na kusaidia nchi nyingi za Afrika kupata uhuru. Alifariki dunia
tarehe 14 Oktoba 1999 huko London, Uingereza.

SHEIKH ABEID AMANI KARUME (1905–1972)

Sheikh Abeid Amani Karume alizaliwa tarehe 4 Agosti 1905 Zanzibar. Alianza
kama baharia mdogo, kisha akawa kiongozi wa wafanyakazi na mwanasiasa.
Alianzisha Chama cha Afro-Shirazi Party (ASP) mwaka 1957 — chama kilichowakilisha
watu wa asili ya Kiafrika Zanzibar.

Tarehe 12 Januari 1964, Karume aliingia madarakani kufuatia Mapinduzi ya
Zanzibar. Akawa Rais wa Zanzibar na Mwenyekiti wa Baraza la Mapinduzi.

Tarehe 22 Aprili 1964, Karume alitia saini Hati za Muungano pamoja na
Nyerere. Akawa Makamu wa Kwanza wa Rais wa Jamhuri ya Muungano wa Tanzania.

Karume alitawala Zanzibar hadi alipofariki dunia tarehe 7 Aprili 1972,
aliposulubiwa na wapinzani wake.

UMUHIMU WA WAASISI KWA VIJANA

Kuwafahamu Nyerere na Karume ni kujua chanzo cha taifa letu. Waliamua
kujenga taifa jipya kwa njia ya ushirikiano, hekima, na maono ya mbali —
mfano mzuri wa uongozi kwa vijana wa Tanzania leo. Mchango wao hauishii
katika historia — unakaa ndani ya muundo wa Tanzania tunayoiishi leo.

FOUNDERS OF THE UNION OF TANGANYIKA AND ZANZIBAR

The Articles of Union were signed on 22 April 1964 by two principal
founders: Julius Kambarage Nyerere and Sheikh Abeid Amani Karume.

JULIUS KAMBARAGE NYERERE (1922–1999)

Julius Kambarage Nyerere was born on 13 April 1922 in Butiama, Mara
Region, Tanganyika. He graduated from the University of Edinburgh,
Scotland — becoming the first Tanganyikan African to do so.

Nyerere founded the Tanganyika African National Union (TANU) in 1954.
TANU led Tanganyika's independence movement until independence was gained
on 9 December 1961. Nyerere became the first Prime Minister, then the
first President after Tanganyika became a Republic in 1962.

On 22 April 1964, Nyerere signed the Articles of Union and became the
first President of the United Republic of Tanzania. He led Tanzania until
1985.

Nyerere was known as "Mwalimu" — meaning Teacher — as he had been a
teacher before entering politics. He championed African unity, human
rights, and social development. He worked tirelessly against apartheid
in South Africa and helped many African nations gain independence. He
died on 14 October 1999 in London, England.

SHEIKH ABEID AMANI KARUME (1905–1972)

Sheikh Abeid Amani Karume was born on 4 August 1905 in Zanzibar. He
started as a young sailor, then became a labour leader and politician.
He founded the Afro-Shirazi Party (ASP) in 1957 — a party representing
Zanzibar's African-descended population.

On 12 January 1964, Karume came to power following the Zanzibar Revolution.
He became President of Zanzibar and Chairman of the Revolutionary Council.

On 22 April 1964, Karume signed the Articles of Union together with
Nyerere. He became the first Vice-President of the United Republic of
Tanzania.

Karume governed Zanzibar until he was assassinated by opponents on
7 April 1972.

WHY THE FOUNDERS MATTER TO YOUTH

Knowing Nyerere and Karume means knowing the roots of our nation. They
chose to build a new country through cooperation, wisdom, and far-sighted
vision — an inspiring example of leadership for Tanzania's youth today.
Their contribution does not end in history — it lives in the structure
of the Tanzania we inhabit today.
