HATI ZA MUUNGANO (22 APRILI 1964)

Hati za Muungano ni hati ya kisheria iliyosainiwa tarehe 22 Aprili 1964
na viongozi wawili: Julius Kambarage Nyerere, Rais wa Tanganyika, na Sheikh
Abeid Amani Karume, Rais wa Zanzibar na Mwenyekiti wa Baraza la Mapinduzi.
Hati hii ndio msingi wa kisheria wa Muungano wa Tanganyika na Zanzibar, na
iliunda Jamhuri ya Muungano wa Tanzania rasmi tarehe 26 Aprili 1964.

VIFUNGU VIKUU VYA HATI ZA MUUNGANO

Hati za Muungano zilikuwa na vifungu vinne vikuu:

Kifungu cha Kwanza — Muungano
Tanganyika na Zanzibar zilikubaliana kuungana na kuunda Jamhuri moja —
Jamhuri ya Muungano wa Tanganyika na Zanzibar.

Kifungu cha Pili — Serikali ya Muungano
Serikali ya Muungano itakuwa na mamlaka juu ya mambo yaliyoainishwa
katika Hati, ikiwa ni pamoja na ulinzi, mambo ya nje, uraia, na sarafu.

Kifungu cha Tatu — Serikali ya Zanzibar
Serikali ya Mapinduzi ya Zanzibar itaendelea kusimamia mambo ya ndani
ya Zanzibar ambayo hayako chini ya mamlaka ya Serikali ya Muungano.

Kifungu cha Nne — Rais wa Kwanza
Julius Nyerere atakuwa Rais wa kwanza wa Jamhuri ya Muungano, na Abeid
Amani Karume atakuwa Makamu wa Kwanza wa Rais.

UMUHIMU WA HATI ZA MUUNGANO

Hati za Muungano ni kama "cheti cha kuzaliwa" cha Tanzania — nyaraka
zinazoonyesha jinsi taifa letu lilivyoanzishwa kwa makubaliano ya amani
na heshima ya pande zote mbili. Hati hizi ziliunda msingi wa kikatiba
ambao hadi leo unasimamia uhusiano kati ya Tanzania Bara na Zanzibar.

Hati hizi zilikuwa na athari kubwa kwa Afrika nzima, zikionyesha kwamba
nchi mbili huru za Afrika zinaweza kuungana kwa njia ya amani na mazungumzo
badala ya vita au nguvu.

THE ARTICLES OF UNION (22 APRIL 1964)

The Articles of Union is the legal document signed on 22 April 1964 by
two leaders: Julius Kambarage Nyerere, President of Tanganyika, and Sheikh
Abeid Amani Karume, President of Zanzibar and Chairman of the Revolutionary
Council. This document is the legal foundation of the Union of Tanganyika
and Zanzibar, and officially created the United Republic of Tanzania on
26 April 1964.

MAIN ARTICLES OF THE ARTICLES OF UNION

The Articles of Union had four main articles:

Article One — The Union
Tanganyika and Zanzibar agreed to unite and form one Republic — the
Republic of Tanganyika and Zanzibar.

Article Two — The Union Government
The Union Government would have authority over matters specified in the
Articles, including defence, foreign affairs, citizenship, and currency.

Article Three — The Zanzibar Government
The Zanzibar Revolutionary Government would continue to manage Zanzibar's
internal affairs that are not under the authority of the Union Government.

Article Four — The First President
Julius Nyerere would be the first President of the United Republic, and
Abeid Amani Karume would be the first Vice-President.

SIGNIFICANCE OF THE ARTICLES OF UNION

The Articles of Union are like Tanzania's "birth certificate" — the
document showing how our nation was founded through peaceful agreement
and mutual respect between both sides. These Articles created a
constitutional foundation that to this day governs the relationship
between Mainland Tanzania and Zanzibar.

These articles had a major impact across Africa, demonstrating that two
independent African nations could unite peacefully through dialogue and
negotiation rather than war or force.
