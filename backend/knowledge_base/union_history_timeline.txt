HISTORIA YA MUUNGANO WA TANGANYIKA NA ZANZIBAR

MANDHARI YA KIHISTORIA

Kabla ya Muungano, Tanganyika ilikuwa chini ya utawala wa Uingereza kama
eneo la kuaminika la Umoja wa Mataifa. Zanzibar ilikuwa chini ya Sultani
wa Kiarabu, ingawa ilikuwa na uhusiano wa kiprotektorate na Uingereza.
Watu wote wawili walikuwa na historia ndefu ya biashara ya pwani ya Bahari
ya Hindi, lugha ya Kiswahili iliyounganisha jamii zao, na ndoto ya pamoja
ya uhuru na maendeleo.

RATIBA YA MATUKIO MUHIMU

9 Desemba 1961
Tanganyika ilipata uhuru wake kutoka kwa Uingereza. Julius Nyerere alikuwa
Waziri Mkuu wa kwanza. Ilikuwa nchi ya kwanza katika Afrika Mashariki
kupata uhuru.

9 Desemba 1962
Tanganyika iligeuzwa kuwa Jamhuri. Julius Nyerere akawa Rais wa kwanza.

10 Desemba 1963
Zanzibar ilipata uhuru wake kutoka kwa Uingereza. Zanzibar ikawa Ufalme
chini ya Sultani Jamshid bin Abdullah.

12 Januari 1964 — MAPINDUZI YA ZANZIBAR
Mapinduzi ya kijeshi yalipindua Sultani wa Zanzibar. John Okello na Chama
cha Afro-Shirazi Party (ASP) waliongoza mapinduzi. Sheikh Abeid Amani
Karume akawa Rais wa Zanzibar na Mwenyekiti wa Baraza la Mapinduzi. Sultani
Jamshid bin Abdullah alilazimika kukimbia nchini.

Januari–Aprili 1964
Mazungumzo ya siri yalifanyika kati ya Julius Nyerere na Abeid Karume
kuhusu uwezekano wa Muungano. Hatua hii ilichochewa na msukosuko wa kisiasa
na shinikizo la Vita Baridi.

22 Aprili 1964 — HATI ZA MUUNGANO ZINASAINIWA
Julius Nyerere na Abeid Amani Karume walitia saini Hati za Muungano. Hati
hii ya kisheria ilikuwa na vifungu vinne vikuu vilivyoelezea msingi wa
Muungano.

26 Aprili 1964 — SIKU YA MUUNGANO
Jamhuri ya Muungano wa Tanganyika na Zanzibar ilitangazwa rasmi. Tarehe hii
sasa inaadhimishwa kila mwaka kama "Siku ya Muungano" (Union Day).

29 Oktoba 1964
Jina la nchi lilibadilishwa kuwa "Jamhuri ya Muungano wa Tanzania" —
likichanganya maneno "Tanganyika" na "Zanzibar."

1965
Uchaguzi wa kwanza wa Bunge la Muungano ulifanyika.

1977
Katiba mpya ya Jamhuri ya Muungano wa Tanzania ilianzishwa.

1984
Mswada wa Haki uliongezwa kwenye Katiba ya Muungano. Katiba mpya ya
Zanzibar ilianzishwa.

1992
Mfumo wa vyama vingi vya siasa ulianzishwa Tanzania.

HISTORY AND TIMELINE OF THE UNION OF TANGANYIKA AND ZANZIBAR

HISTORICAL BACKGROUND

Before the Union, Tanganyika was under British administration as a UN
trust territory. Zanzibar was under an Arab Sultanate with a British
protectorate relationship. Both peoples shared a long history of Indian
Ocean coastal trade, the Swahili language that connected their communities,
and a common dream of independence and development.

KEY EVENTS TIMELINE

9 December 1961
Tanganyika gained independence from Britain. Julius Nyerere became the
first Prime Minister. It was the first country in East Africa to gain
independence.

9 December 1962
Tanganyika became a Republic. Julius Nyerere became the first President.

10 December 1963
Zanzibar gained independence from Britain. Zanzibar became a Kingdom
under Sultan Jamshid bin Abdullah.

12 January 1964 — THE ZANZIBAR REVOLUTION
An armed revolution overthrew the Sultanate of Zanzibar. John Okello and
the Afro-Shirazi Party (ASP) led the revolution. Sheikh Abeid Amani Karume
became President of Zanzibar and Chairman of the Revolutionary Council.
Sultan Jamshid bin Abdullah was forced to flee the country.

January–April 1964
Secret talks took place between Julius Nyerere and Abeid Karume about
the possibility of a Union. This was driven by political instability and
Cold War pressures.

22 April 1964 — ARTICLES OF UNION SIGNED
Julius Nyerere and Abeid Amani Karume signed the Articles of Union. This
legal document had four main articles laying out the foundation of the Union.

26 April 1964 — UNION DAY
The Republic of Tanganyika and Zanzibar was officially proclaimed. This
date is now celebrated every year as "Union Day" (Siku ya Muungano).

29 October 1964
The country was renamed "The United Republic of Tanzania" — combining
"Tanganyika" and "Zanzibar."

1965
The first elections for the Union Parliament were held.

1977
A new Constitution of the United Republic of Tanzania was enacted.

1984
A Bill of Rights was added to the Union Constitution. A new Zanzibar
Constitution was enacted.

1992
A multi-party political system was introduced in Tanzania.
