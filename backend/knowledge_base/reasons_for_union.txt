SABABU ZA MUUNGANO WA TANGANYIKA NA ZANZIBAR (1964)

Muungano wa Tanganyika na Zanzibar mwaka 1964 ulisababishwa na mchanganyiko
wa haja ya kisiasa, usalama, maadili ya Kiafrika ya pamoja, na historia
ya pamoja. Hapa chini ni sababu kuu zilizoleta Muungano:

1. MAPINDUZI YA ZANZIBAR (JANUARI 1964)

Tarehe 12 Januari 1964, mapinduzi ya kijeshi yalifanyika Zanzibar na
kupindua utawala wa Sultani Jamshid bin Abdullah. Mapinduzi hayo yaliongozwa
na John Okello na Chama cha Afro-Shirazi Party (ASP). Serikali mpya ya
Mapinduzi ilianzishwa chini ya Sheikh Abeid Amani Karume. Msukosuko
mkubwa wa kisiasa uliofuata mapinduzi hayo ulifanya iwe wazi kwamba
Zanzibar ilihitaji msaada na utulivu.

2. SHINIKIZO LA VITA BARIDI

Wakati wa Vita Baridi kati ya Marekani na Umoja wa Kisovyeti, Zanzibar
iliangaliwa kwa wasiwasi mkubwa na nguvu za Magharibi. Serikali mpya ya
Mapinduzi ya Zanzibar ilionyesha ishara za ushirikiano na Cuba na Umoja
wa Kisovyeti. Marekani na Uingereza waliogopa Zanzibar kuwa kambi ya
ukomunisti katikati ya Bahari ya Hindi. Julius Nyerere wa Tanganyika
aliona Muungano kama njia bora ya kulinda eneo hili na kuzuia uingiliaji
wa nguvu za kigeni.

3. MAADILI YA PAN-AFRICANISM

Nyerere alikuwa mwamini mkubwa wa umoja wa Afrika — wazo la Pan-Africanism
lililokuwa likisambaa kote Afrika wakati huo. Muungano wa Tanganyika na
Zanzibar ulionekana kama hatua ya vitendo kuelekea ndoto ya Afrika
iliyounganika. Karume pia alishiriki maadili hayo ya umoja wa Kiafrika.

4. HISTORIA NA LUGHA YA PAMOJA

Tanganyika na Zanzibar zilikuwa na historia ndefu ya mahusiano ya kibiashara
na kijamii, haswa kupitia biashara ya pwani ya Bahari ya Hindi. Lugha ya
Kiswahili — iliyokuwa chanzo cha Zanzibar — iliunganisha watu wa pande
zote mbili. Historia hii ya pamoja ilifanya Muungano kuwa wa asili zaidi.

5. NGUVU ZA PAMOJA NA USALAMA

Nchi iliyounganika ingekuwa na nguvu zaidi katika mazungumzo ya kimataifa.
Muungano ungeipa nchi sauti ya nguvu zaidi katika Umoja wa Afrika na katika
mazungumzo ya kimataifa. Pia, muundo wa ulinzi wa pamoja ungekuwa imara
zaidi dhidi ya vitisho vya nje.

MATOKEO

Hati za Muungano zilisainiwa tarehe 22 Aprili 1964 na Julius Nyerere
(Rais wa Tanganyika) na Sheikh Abeid Amani Karume (Rais wa Zanzibar).
Tarehe 26 Aprili 1964 ikawa siku rasmi ya kuzaliwa kwa Jamhuri ya Muungano
wa Tanzania — siku inayoadhimishwa kila mwaka kama Siku ya Muungano.

REASONS FOR THE UNION OF TANGANYIKA AND ZANZIBAR (1964)

The Union of Tanganyika and Zanzibar in 1964 was driven by a combination
of political necessity, security concerns, shared African ideals, and
common history. The key reasons:

1. THE ZANZIBAR REVOLUTION (JANUARY 1964)

On 12 January 1964, an armed revolution in Zanzibar overthrew the Sultanate
of Sultan Jamshid bin Abdullah. Led by John Okello and the Afro-Shirazi
Party (ASP), the revolution established a new Revolutionary Government
under Sheikh Abeid Amani Karume. The political instability that followed
made it clear that Zanzibar needed stability and support.

2. COLD WAR PRESSURES

During the Cold War between the USA and Soviet Union, Zanzibar was watched
with great concern by Western powers. The new Zanzibar Revolutionary
Government showed signs of aligning with Cuba and the Soviet Union. The
USA and Britain feared Zanzibar becoming a communist base in the Indian
Ocean. Julius Nyerere of Tanganyika saw the Union as the best way to
stabilise the region and prevent foreign interference.

3. PAN-AFRICAN IDEALS

Nyerere was a committed Pan-Africanist — a belief in African unity that
was spreading across the continent at the time. The Union of Tanganyika
and Zanzibar was seen as a practical step toward the dream of a united
Africa. Karume also shared these ideals of African unity.

4. SHARED HISTORY AND LANGUAGE

Tanganyika and Zanzibar had a long history of trade and social relations,
especially through Indian Ocean coastal trade. The Swahili language —
which originated from Zanzibar — connected the people of both sides.
This shared history made the Union more natural.

5. COMBINED STRENGTH AND SECURITY

A united nation would be stronger in international negotiations. The Union
would give the country a more powerful voice in the African Union and in
global discussions. Also, a joint defence structure would be more resilient
against external threats.

RESULT

The Articles of Union were signed on 22 April 1964 by Julius Nyerere
(President of Tanganyika) and Sheikh Abeid Amani Karume (President of
Zanzibar). 26 April 1964 became the official birthday of the United
Republic of Tanzania — a date celebrated every year as Union Day.
