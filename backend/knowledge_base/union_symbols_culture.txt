ALAMA NA UTAMADUNI WA MUUNGANO WA TANZANIA

BENDERA YA TANZANIA

Bendera ya Tanzania ina rangi nne:
- Kijani (juu kushoto): Inawakilisha mandhari ya asili ya Tanzania
- Njano (mstari mwembamba): Inawakilisha madini na utajiri wa ardhi
- Nyeusi (mstari mpana katikati): Inawakilisha watu wa Tanzania
- Bluu (chini kulia): Inawakilisha bahari, maziwa, na mito ya Tanzania

Bendera ilianzishwa mwaka 1964 kama alama ya Jamhuri ya Muungano mpya.

WIMBO WA TAIFA

Wimbo wa taifa wa Tanzania ni "Mungu Ibariki Afrika" (God Bless Africa),
unaojulikana pia kama "Nkosi Sikelel' iAfrika" kwa lugha ya Nguni.
Wimbo huu ulianza kutumiwa Tanzania mwaka 1961 na bado unatumika kama
wimbo wa taifa wa Muungano.

SIKU ZA KITAIFA ZA MUUNGANO

Siku ya Muungano (26 Aprili): Inaadhimisha kuzaliwa kwa Jamhuri ya
Muungano wa Tanzania tarehe 26 Aprili 1964. Ni siku ya sherehe kubwa
inayoadhimishwa kote Tanzania.

Siku ya Uhuru (9 Desemba): Inaadhimisha uhuru wa Tanganyika tarehe
9 Desemba 1961.

Siku ya Mapinduzi ya Zanzibar (12 Januari): Inaadhimisha Mapinduzi ya
Zanzibar tarehe 12 Januari 1964.

LUGHA YA KISWAHILI

Kiswahili ni lugha ya taifa ya Tanzania — lugha inayounganisha Watanzania
wote wa Tanzania Bara na Zanzibar. Kiswahili kilikuwa chanzo cha Zanzibar,
na Muungano ulisaidia kueneza lugha hii kote Tanzania Bara. Leo Kiswahili
ni lugha ya pili ya Afrika inayozungumzwa zaidi, baada ya Kiarabu.

NEMBO YA TAIFA

Nembo ya Tanzania inaonyesha:
- Ngao na mkuki: Ulinzi na nguvu
- Mlima Kilimanjaro: Maliasili ya Tanzania
- Mawimbi ya bahari: Zanzibar na pwani
- Bonde la Ufa: Mandhari ya Tanzania Bara
- Wanyama (Simba): Nguvu na utukufu

UNION SYMBOLS AND CULTURE OF TANZANIA

THE TANZANIAN FLAG

The Tanzanian flag has four colours:
- Green (upper left): Represents Tanzania's natural landscape
- Yellow (thin stripe): Represents minerals and the wealth of the land
- Black (wide central stripe): Represents the people of Tanzania
- Blue (lower right): Represents Tanzania's ocean, lakes, and rivers

The flag was introduced in 1964 as a symbol of the new United Republic.

NATIONAL ANTHEM

Tanzania's national anthem is "Mungu Ibariki Afrika" (God Bless Africa),
also known as "Nkosi Sikelel' iAfrika" in the Nguni language. This song
began to be used in Tanzania in 1961 and continues to serve as the
Union's national anthem.

NATIONAL UNION HOLIDAYS

Union Day (26 April): Celebrates the birth of the United Republic of
Tanzania on 26 April 1964. It is a major national celebration observed
across Tanzania.

Independence Day (9 December): Celebrates Tanganyika's independence
on 9 December 1961.

Zanzibar Revolution Day (12 January): Celebrates the Zanzibar Revolution
of 12 January 1964.

THE SWAHILI LANGUAGE

Swahili is Tanzania's national language — the language that unites all
Tanzanians from Mainland Tanzania and Zanzibar. Swahili originated in
Zanzibar, and the Union helped spread this language across Mainland
Tanzania. Today Swahili is the second most widely spoken language in
Africa after Arabic.

THE NATIONAL COAT OF ARMS

Tanzania's coat of arms shows:
- Shield and spear: Protection and strength
- Mount Kilimanjaro: Tanzania's natural resources
- Ocean waves: Zanzibar and the coast
- The Rift Valley: Mainland Tanzania's landscape
- Animals (Lion): Strength and glory
