MUUNGANO WA TANGANYIKA NA ZANZIBAR NI NINI?

Muungano ni makubaliano rasmi ya kisiasa na kikatiba ambapo nchi mbili au
zaidi zinazokuwa huru zinakubali kuachia mamlaka yao yote au sehemu yake
kwa serikali moja kuu. Serikali hiyo kuu inatekeleza mamlaka hayo chini ya
masharti maalum ya kikatiba.

MUUNGANO WA TANZANIA

Jamhuri ya Muungano wa Tanzania iliundwa tarehe 26 Aprili 1964 wakati
Tanganyika na Zanzibar zilipounganika kuwa nchi moja. Muungano huu uliundwa
kupitia Hati za Muungano — hati ya kisheria iliyosainiwa na Julius Nyerere
(Rais wa Tanganyika) na Abeid Amani Karume (Rais wa Zanzibar) tarehe 22
Aprili 1964.

MUUNDO WA MUUNGANO

Muungano wa Tanzania una mfumo wa serikali mbili:

1. Serikali ya Jamhuri ya Muungano — inayosimamia mambo yanayoathiri pande
   zote mbili za Tanzania (Tanzania Bara na Zanzibar). Mambo hayo yanaitwa
   "Mambo ya Muungano" na kuna jumla ya mambo 22 yaliyoainishwa katika
   Katiba.

2. Serikali ya Mapinduzi ya Zanzibar — inayosimamia mambo ya ndani ya
   Zanzibar ambayo si sehemu ya Mambo ya Muungano.

Tanzania Bara haijui serikali yake tofauti — inasimamia mambo yake kupitia
Serikali ya Muungano.

JINA LA NCHI

Awali, nchi iliitwa "Jamhuri ya Muungano wa Tanganyika na Zanzibar." Tarehe
29 Oktoba 1964, jina lilibadilishwa kuwa "Jamhuri ya Muungano wa Tanzania"
— jina ambalo limechanganya maneno ya "Tanganyika" na "Zanzibar."

UMUHIMU WA MUUNGANO KWA VIJANA

Kuelewa Muungano kunamaanisha kuelewa ni nani wewe kama raia wa Tanzania.
Muungano si muundo wa kisiasa tu — unawakilisha utambulisho wa pamoja, amani,
na umoja wa Watanzania zaidi ya milioni 60. Kama vijana, nyinyi ndio walinzi
wa Muungano huu na mnawajibika kulinda maadili ya ushirikiano na umoja wa
kitaifa.

WHAT IS THE UNION OF TANGANYIKA AND ZANZIBAR?

A union is a formal political and constitutional arrangement where two or
more independent nations agree to surrender all or part of their sovereignty
to a single central government. That central government exercises authority
under specific constitutional conditions.

THE UNION OF TANZANIA

The United Republic of Tanzania was officially formed on 26 April 1964 when
Tanganyika and Zanzibar merged into one country. The Union was created
through the Articles of Union — a legal document signed by Julius Nyerere
(President of Tanganyika) and Abeid Amani Karume (President of Zanzibar)
on 22 April 1964.

STRUCTURE OF THE UNION

Tanzania's Union operates a two-government structure:

1. The Government of the United Republic — overseeing matters that affect
   both sides of Tanzania (Mainland Tanzania and Zanzibar). These are called
   "Union Matters" and there are 22 of them listed in the Constitution.

2. The Zanzibar Revolutionary Government — overseeing Zanzibar's internal
   affairs that are not part of Union Matters.

Mainland Tanzania does not have its own separate government — its affairs
are managed through the Union Government.

NAME OF THE COUNTRY

Initially the country was called "The Republic of Tanganyika and Zanzibar."
On 29 October 1964, the name was changed to "The United Republic of
Tanzania" — a name combining "Tanganyika" and "Zanzibar."

WHY THE UNION MATTERS TO YOUTH

Understanding the Union means understanding who you are as a Tanzanian
citizen. The Union is not just a political structure — it represents the
shared identity, peace, and unity of over 60 million Tanzanians. As youth,
you are the guardians of this Union and responsible for preserving its
values of cooperation and national unity.
