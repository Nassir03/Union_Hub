MAPINDUZI YA ZANZIBAR (12 JANUARI 1964)

MANDHARI YA KIHISTORIA

Kabla ya Mapinduzi, Zanzibar ilikuwa chini ya utawala wa Sultani wa Kiarabu.
Sultani Jamshid bin Abdullah alitawala kama mkuu wa nchi baada ya uhuru wa
Zanzibar tarehe 10 Desemba 1963. Jamii ya Zanzibar ilikuwa imegawanyika
kwa nguvu kati ya watu wa asili ya Kiarabu na watu wa asili ya Kiafrika.
Watu wengi wa asili ya Kiafrika walikuwa wakikabiliwa na ukosefu wa haki
za kisiasa, kiuchumi, na kijamii chini ya utawala huo.

VIONGOZI WA MAPINDUZI

Mapinduzi yaliongozwa na:
- John Okello: Kiongozi mkuu wa kijeshi wa Mapinduzi. Alizaliwa Uganda,
  alikuwa amekaa Zanzibar kwa muda mfupi. Alitangazwa kama "Field Marshal"
  wa Mapinduzi.
- Sheikh Abeid Amani Karume: Kiongozi wa Chama cha Afro-Shirazi Party (ASP),
  chama kilichowakilisha watu wa asili ya Kiafrika Zanzibar.
- Abdulrahman Muhammad Babu: Kiongozi wa Umma Party na mkuu wa harakati
  ya kikomunisti Zanzibar.

JINSI MAPINDUZI YALIVYOFANYIKA

Tarehe 12 Januari 1964, kabla ya alfajiri, kikosi cha wapiganaji wapatao
600-800 walishambulia maeneo muhimu ya Zanzibar Town. Walishambulia
makao makuu ya polisi, ghala za silaha, na radio ya taifa. Ndani ya saa
chache, Sultani Jamshid bin Abdullah alilazimika kukimbia nchini akienda
uhamishoni. Mwisho wa siku hiyo, udhibiti kamili wa Zanzibar ulikuwa
mikononi mwa wapiganaji.

SERIKALI MPYA YA MAPINDUZI

Baada ya Mapinduzi, Serikali ya Mapinduzi ya Watu wa Zanzibar ilianzishwa:
- Sheikh Abeid Amani Karume akawa Rais wa Zanzibar
- Baraza la Mapinduzi likaundwa kuwa chombo kikuu cha utawala
- Ardhi ya wamiliki wa Kiarabu ilichukuliwa na kugawanywa
- Shughuli nyingi za biashara zilitaifishwa
- Vyama vingi vya siasa viliachwa, na ASP na Umma Party viliungana

ATHARI ZA MAPINDUZI KWA MUUNGANO

Mapinduzi ya Zanzibar yalikuwa moja ya sababu kuu zilizosababisha Muungano
wa Tanganyika na Zanzibar Aprili 1964. Msukosuko wa kisiasa uliofuata
Mapinduzi — pamoja na wasiwasi wa nguvu za Magharibi kuhusu ushawishi wa
ukomunisti — ulifanya mazungumzo ya Muungano kuwa ya haraka zaidi.

UMUHIMU KWA VIJANA

Mapinduzi ya Zanzibar ni sehemu muhimu ya historia ya Tanzania ambayo
haiwezi kukaa pembeni. Kuelewa tukio hili kunasaidia kuelewa jinsi Muungano
ulivyotokea, changamoto ambazo viongozi walikabiliana nazo, na muktadha
wa kihistoria wa Tanzania ya leo.

THE ZANZIBAR REVOLUTION (12 JANUARY 1964)

HISTORICAL BACKGROUND

Before the Revolution, Zanzibar was under an Arab Sultanate. Sultan
Jamshid bin Abdullah ruled as head of state after Zanzibar's independence
on 10 December 1963. Zanzibar's society was sharply divided between
people of Arab descent and people of African descent. Many of African
descent faced political, economic, and social inequality under this rule.

LEADERS OF THE REVOLUTION

The Revolution was led by:
- John Okello: The principal military leader of the Revolution. Born in
  Uganda, he had lived in Zanzibar for a short time. He declared himself
  "Field Marshal" of the Revolution.
- Sheikh Abeid Amani Karume: Leader of the Afro-Shirazi Party (ASP),
  the party representing Zanzibar's African-descended population.
- Abdulrahman Muhammad Babu: Leader of the Umma Party and head of
  Zanzibar's communist movement.

HOW THE REVOLUTION UNFOLDED

On 12 January 1964, before dawn, a force of approximately 600-800 fighters
attacked key locations in Zanzibar Town. They attacked police headquarters,
weapons stores, and the national radio station. Within hours, Sultan
Jamshid bin Abdullah was forced to flee the country into exile. By the
end of that day, full control of Zanzibar was in the hands of the fighters.

THE NEW REVOLUTIONARY GOVERNMENT

After the Revolution, the People's Revolutionary Government of Zanzibar
was established:
- Sheikh Abeid Amani Karume became President of Zanzibar
- A Revolutionary Council was formed as the supreme governing body
- Arab landowners' land was seized and redistributed
- Many businesses were nationalised
- Multiple political parties were disbanded; ASP and Umma Party merged

IMPACT OF THE REVOLUTION ON THE UNION

The Zanzibar Revolution was one of the key reasons that led to the Union
of Tanganyika and Zanzibar in April 1964. The political instability that
followed the Revolution — along with Western concerns about communist
influence — made Union negotiations more urgent.

WHY IT MATTERS TO YOUTH

The Zanzibar Revolution is an important part of Tanzania's history that
cannot be set aside. Understanding this event helps explain how the Union
came about, the challenges leaders faced, and the historical context of
today's Tanzania.
