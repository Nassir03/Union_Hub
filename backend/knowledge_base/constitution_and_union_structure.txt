KATIBA YA JAMHURI YA MUUNGANO WA TANZANIA

Katiba ya Jamhuri ya Muungano wa Tanzania ya 1977 ni sheria kuu ya nchi.
Inaorodhesha mambo ya Muungano, haki za raia, na muundo wa taasisi za
Serikali ya Muungano.

HISTORIA YA KATIBA ZA TANZANIA

1964 — Katiba ya Muda
Katiba ya kwanza ya Muungano iliandikwa mwaka 1964 baada ya Hati za
Muungano kusainiwa. Iliweka misingi ya awali ya Muungano.

1965 — Katiba mpya
Katiba mpya ilianzishwa na mfumo wa chama kimoja — TANU ukiwa chama
pekee Tanzania Bara na ASP ukiwa chama pekee Zanzibar.

1977 — Katiba ya CCM
Vyama vya TANU na ASP viliunganika na kuunda Chama cha Mapinduzi (CCM).
Katiba mpya iliandikwa na kuanza kutumika. Katiba hii ya 1977 ndiyo
inayotumika hadi leo (pamoja na marekebisho mengi).

1984 — Mswada wa Haki
Mabadiliko muhimu ya Katiba yaliongeza Sehemu ya Tatu — Mswada wa Haki —
unaohakikisha haki za msingi za binadamu kwa raia wote wa Tanzania.

1992 — Mfumo wa Vyama Vingi
Mabadiliko ya Katiba yaliruhusu mfumo wa vyama vingi vya siasa.
Tanzania ilianzisha uchaguzi wa vyama vingi kuanzia 1995.

2000s — Marekebisho Mbalimbali
Katiba imebadilishwa mara nyingi ili kushughulikia mahitaji mapya ya
kisiasa, kiuchumi, na kijamii.

MUUNDO WA SERIKALI WA MUUNGANO

Kwa mujibu wa Katiba, Serikali ya Muungano ina nguzo kuu tatu:

NGUZO YA KWANZA: BUNGE (LEGISLATIVE)
Bunge la Jamhuri ya Muungano lina mamlaka ya kutunga sheria za Muungano.
Lina wabunge waliochaguliwa kutoka majimbo yote ya Tanzania Bara na
Zanzibar. Kuna pia viti vya wabunge wa kuteua kwa wanawake.

NGUZO YA PILI: SERIKALI (EXECUTIVE)
Rais wa Jamhuri ya Muungano ndiye mkuu wa serikali na mkuu wa nchi.
Rais anachaguliwa na wananchi moja kwa moja. Waziri Mkuu anasaidia Rais
kusimamia shughuli za Bunge. Baraza la Mawaziri linasaidia Rais kutekeleza
sera za serikali.

NGUZO YA TATU: MAHAKAMA (JUDICIARY)
Mahakama ya Rufani ya Tanzania ndiyo mahakama ya juu zaidi ya Muungano.
Mahakama ya Juu ya Tanzania inashughulikia kesi za Tanzania Bara. Haki
za kikatiba za raia zinalindwa na mfumo huu wa mahakama.

MUUNDO WA SERIKALI YA ZANZIBAR

Zanzibar ina muundo wake wa serikali ndani ya mfumo wa Muungano:
- Rais wa Zanzibar: Mkuu wa Serikali ya Mapinduzi
- Baraza la Mapinduzi: Chombo cha juu cha utawala
- Baraza la Wawakilishi: Bunge la Zanzibar
- Mahakama ya Juu ya Zanzibar

THE CONSTITUTION OF THE UNITED REPUBLIC OF TANZANIA

The 1977 Constitution of the United Republic of Tanzania is the supreme
law of the land. It lists Union Matters, citizens' rights, and the
institutional structure of the Union Government.

HISTORY OF TANZANIA'S CONSTITUTIONS

1964 — Interim Constitution
The first Union Constitution was written in 1964 after the Articles of
Union were signed. It laid the initial foundations of the Union.

1965 — New Constitution
A new Constitution established a single-party system — TANU as the sole
party in Mainland Tanzania and ASP as the sole party in Zanzibar.

1977 — CCM Constitution
TANU and ASP merged to form Chama cha Mapinduzi (CCM). A new Constitution
was written and came into force. This 1977 Constitution is still in use
today (with many amendments).

1984 — Bill of Rights
A major Constitutional amendment added Part Three — the Bill of Rights —
guaranteeing fundamental human rights for all Tanzanian citizens.

1992 — Multi-Party System
Constitutional amendments allowed a multi-party political system.
Tanzania introduced multi-party elections starting in 1995.

2000s — Various Amendments
The Constitution has been amended multiple times to address new political,
economic, and social needs.

STRUCTURE OF UNION GOVERNMENT

Under the Constitution, the Union Government has three main pillars:

FIRST PILLAR: PARLIAMENT (LEGISLATIVE)
The Parliament of the United Republic has authority to make Union laws.
It includes MPs elected from all constituencies across Mainland Tanzania
and Zanzibar. There are also reserved seats for women MPs.

SECOND PILLAR: GOVERNMENT (EXECUTIVE)
The President of the United Republic is both head of government and head
of state. The President is directly elected by citizens. The Prime
Minister assists the President in managing Parliamentary business. The
Cabinet assists the President in implementing government policies.

THIRD PILLAR: COURTS (JUDICIARY)
The Court of Appeal of Tanzania is the highest Union court. The High
Court of Tanzania handles Mainland Tanzania cases. Citizens' constitutional
rights are protected by this court system.

STRUCTURE OF ZANZIBAR GOVERNMENT

Zanzibar has its own government structure within the Union framework:
- President of Zanzibar: Head of the Revolutionary Government
- Revolutionary Council: Supreme governing body
- House of Representatives: Zanzibar's Parliament
- High Court of Zanzibar
