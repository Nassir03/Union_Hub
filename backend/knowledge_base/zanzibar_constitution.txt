KATIBA YA ZANZIBAR YA 1984

Katiba ya Zanzibar ya 1984 ni sheria kuu ya Zanzibar inayosimamia mfumo
wa Serikali ya Mapinduzi ya Zanzibar na Baraza la Wawakilishi ndani ya
muundo mpana wa Jamhuri ya Muungano wa Tanzania.

HISTORIA YA KATIBA YA ZANZIBAR

Baada ya Mapinduzi ya 1964, Zanzibar iliongozwa kupitia miongozo ya
Baraza la Mapinduzi bila katiba rasmi. Mwaka 1979, Baraza la Mapinduzi
lilianzisha mchakato wa kuandika katiba rasmi. Tarehe 12 Januari 1984,
siku ya miaka 20 ya Mapinduzi, Katiba ya Zanzibar ya 1984 ilitangazwa
na kuanza kutumika.

MAUDHUI YA KATIBA YA ZANZIBAR YA 1984

Katiba ya Zanzibar ya 1984 inaeleza:

1. MUUNDO WA SERIKALI YA ZANZIBAR
- Rais wa Zanzibar: Mkuu wa Serikali ya Mapinduzi, anachaguliwa na
  wananchi wa Zanzibar
- Baraza la Wawakilishi: Bunge la Zanzibar lenye mamlaka ya kutunga
  sheria za mambo ya Zanzibar
- Baraza la Mawaziri: Inayosaidia Rais kutekeleza sera
- Mahakama ya Juu ya Zanzibar

2. MAMBO YA ZANZIBAR
Zanzibar inasimamia mambo yake yote ya ndani ambayo hayako kwenye orodha
ya Mambo ya Muungano. Hii inajumuisha: afya, elimu ya msingi na sekondari,
kilimo, uvuvi, ardhi, na huduma nyingi za kijamii.

3. HAKI ZA RAIA WA ZANZIBAR
Katiba inalinda haki za msingi za raia wa Zanzibar, ikiwemo haki ya elimu,
afya, makazi, na ushiriki wa kisiasa.

4. UHUSIANO NA MUUNGANO
Katiba ya Zanzibar inakubali na kuheshimu mfumo wa Muungano. Inabainisha
kwamba Zanzibar ni sehemu ya Jamhuri ya Muungano wa Tanzania na kwamba
mambo ya Muungano yanafuata Katiba ya Muungano.

MAREKEBISHO YA KATIBA YA ZANZIBAR

Katiba ya Zanzibar ya 1984 imebadilishwa mara kadhaa:
- 1992: Mabadiliko yaliruhusu mfumo wa vyama vingi Zanzibar
- 2010: Mabadiliko makubwa yaliyoundwa kupitia Serikali ya Umoja wa Kitaifa
  (GNU) — ilianzisha Rais wa pili wa Zanzibar (mmoja kutoka CCM na mmoja
  kutoka CUF)
- 2020: Marekebisho zaidi yaliyofanywa

TOFAUTI NA KATIBA YA MUUNGANO

Ni muhimu kuelewa tofauti:
- Katiba ya Muungano (1977) — inasimamia Mambo ya Muungano na taifa
  lote la Tanzania
- Katiba ya Zanzibar (1984) — inasimamia mambo ya ndani ya Zanzibar
  tu, ndani ya mfumo wa Muungano

THE ZANZIBAR CONSTITUTION OF 1984

The 1984 Zanzibar Constitution is the supreme law of Zanzibar governing
the structure of the Zanzibar Revolutionary Government and House of
Representatives within the broader framework of the United Republic
of Tanzania.

HISTORY OF THE ZANZIBAR CONSTITUTION

After the 1964 Revolution, Zanzibar was governed through Revolutionary
Council decrees without a formal constitution. In 1979, the Revolutionary
Council initiated a process to write a formal constitution. On 12 January
1984 — the 20th anniversary of the Revolution — the 1984 Zanzibar
Constitution was proclaimed and came into force.

CONTENT OF THE 1984 ZANZIBAR CONSTITUTION

The 1984 Zanzibar Constitution describes:

1. STRUCTURE OF ZANZIBAR'S GOVERNMENT
- President of Zanzibar: Head of the Revolutionary Government, elected
  by Zanzibar's citizens
- House of Representatives: Zanzibar's Parliament with authority to make
  laws for Zanzibar's matters
- Cabinet: Assists the President in implementing policies
- High Court of Zanzibar

2. ZANZIBAR'S MATTERS
Zanzibar manages all its internal matters that are not on the list of
Union Matters. This includes: health, primary and secondary education,
agriculture, fishing, land, and many social services.

3. RIGHTS OF ZANZIBAR CITIZENS
The Constitution protects fundamental rights of Zanzibar citizens,
including the right to education, health, housing, and political
participation.

4. RELATIONSHIP WITH THE UNION
The Zanzibar Constitution recognises and respects the Union framework.
It clarifies that Zanzibar is part of the United Republic of Tanzania
and that Union matters follow the Union Constitution.

AMENDMENTS TO THE ZANZIBAR CONSTITUTION

The 1984 Zanzibar Constitution has been amended several times:
- 1992: Amendments allowed a multi-party system in Zanzibar
- 2010: Major amendments created through the Government of National Unity
  (GNU) — established a second President of Zanzibar (one from CCM and
  one from CUF)
- 2020: Further amendments were made

DISTINCTION FROM THE UNION CONSTITUTION

It is important to understand the distinction:
- The Union Constitution (1977) — governs Union Matters and all of Tanzania
- The Zanzibar Constitution (1984) — governs Zanzibar's internal matters
  only, within the Union framework
